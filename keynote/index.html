The third reason is that I've worked with a lot of staff at a lot of libraries and staff often feel like the only factor that plays a part in the ILS they're using is luck. A lot of staff feel like they have no control over the features in their ILS because they're often not the people who make decisions about the ILS.

Library!

I worked for Boise Public Library from 1997-2002.

I know I felt that way when I was using the last proprietary ILS I was on. We could talk to support staff at that company that produced that softare and say "What if the system did ___" but no one ever listened to us.

Listen by e-mail

Koha community mailing lists

Any staff member from any library can join the Koha mailing list and read those emails. Getting involved in the Koha community is as simple as that.

koha-US Collaborate page

Tom Petty was wrong

The hardest part for most front line staff of becoming active in the Koha community is that you actually have to do some work to be a part of it. But it's all fairly easy work.

An Example of My Workflow

Go to Bugzilla

So here's a quick example of what I do once or twice a week. I go to Bugzilla and click on the "Bugs reported in the last 7 days" or "Bugs changed in the last 7 days" link.

List of bugs

This is where the action is

This gets me a list of what's going on. These are new bugs, bugs that have had patches written for them, bugs that need signoff, bugs that just passed QA, etc. This is where the action is. So I browse through the list of bugs. And if I see any that look important to me,

Read them

I read them.
It's as simple as that. I try to take some time every week to read through a list of bugs on Bugzilla and I try to read the ones that I think might impact me.

Sign off

Yeah, I'm not going to talk about that today.

Sure, sometimes I sign off on them, but I'm not going to go into signing off on a patch in this presentation. That's for another program. But just going through and reading the bugs and the comments helps me know what's going on in Koha this week.

A Really Simple Example

Last July I was doing reading through the recent bugs and I came across bug 23389. The title was "Add 'All' option to report value dropdowns." And I thought, hey, I have a work-around for that that I've been using for years. This is cool.

Nick wrote a patch

I read the comments and the test plan and I immediately thought - woah - this is not what I want. I have reports in my system with drop-downs that, if I ran them with an "All" option, they'd time out.

I commented

So, I made a comment. I like the idea, but can you make it optional because I don't want it on all of my reports.

Nick changed the patch and addressed my comments

And a day later Nick added a new patch that had did the same thing, but "All" was optional.

Discussions are Good

Having these kinds of discussions is what the developers want. The developers want to make Koha work better. But they can't do that without feedback from us.
